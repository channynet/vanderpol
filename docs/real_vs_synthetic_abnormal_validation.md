# Real vs Synthetic Abnormal ECG Validation

This validation compares real MIT-BIH abnormal ECG windows against synthetic
abnormal ECG observations generated by the simulator.

## Inputs

- Real ECG: `outputs/mitdb_abnormal_windows.csv`
- Synthetic ECG: simulator-generated 4-second observations
- Synthetic samples per scenario: `200`

## Matched Comparison Groups

| Comparison group | Real MIT-BIH labels | Synthetic scenario |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `svt_afl_vs_svt_flutter` | `SVT`, `AFL` | `svt_flutter` |
| `vt_vs_monomorphic_vt` | `VT` | `monomorphic_vt` |
| `vf_vfl_vs_vf_like` | `VF` / `VFL` | `vf_like` |

The following real MIT-BIH labels currently have no matched synthetic scenario:

- `AFIB` (`337` windows)
- `VENTRICULAR_BIGEMINY` (`324` windows)
- `VENTRICULAR_TRIGEMINY` (`78` windows)
- `IVR` (`5` windows)
- `NODAL` (`51` windows)

## Outputs

- Distance table: `outputs/comparison/real_vs_synthetic_abnormal_feature_distances.csv`
- Group summary table: `outputs/comparison/real_vs_synthetic_abnormal_group_summary.csv`
- Unmatched labels: `outputs/comparison/real_abnormal_unmatched_labels.csv`
- Manifest: `outputs/comparison/real_vs_synthetic_abnormal_manifest.json`
- SMD heatmap: `outputs/comparison/real_vs_synthetic_smd_heatmap.png`
- KS heatmap: `outputs/comparison/real_vs_synthetic_ks_heatmap.png`
- PCA plot: `outputs/comparison/real_vs_synthetic_feature_pca.png`

## Metrics

- `smd_abs`: absolute standardized mean difference. Lower is better; values
  above about `1` indicate large mean separation.
- `ks_statistic`: empirical distribution separation. `0` is identical, `1` is
  completely separated.
- `wasserstein_distance`: average one-dimensional feature distance in original
  units.

## Current Findings

The current synthetic abnormal rhythms are not yet close enough to real MIT-BIH
abnormal ECG in feature space.

Largest gaps:

| Comparison | Feature | Real mean | Synthetic mean | SMD abs | KS |
| --- | --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| `vt_vs_monomorphic_vt` | `spectral_entropy` | 0.669 | 0.209 | 5.506 | 1.000 |
| `vt_vs_monomorphic_vt` | `qrs_width_s` | 0.071 | 0.215 | 5.150 | 0.965 |
| `vf_vfl_vs_vf_like` | `sample_entropy` | 0.596 | 1.500 | 4.842 | 0.995 |
| `vt_vs_monomorphic_vt` | `sample_entropy` | 0.265 | 0.963 | 3.386 | 0.942 |
| `vf_vfl_vs_vf_like` | `dominant_frequency_hz` | 2.857 | 7.990 | 2.752 | 1.000 |
| `svt_afl_vs_svt_flutter` | `spectral_entropy` | 0.758 | 0.519 | 2.688 | 0.895 |
| `svt_afl_vs_svt_flutter` | `rr_cv` | 0.270 | 0.015 | 2.350 | 0.900 |
| `svt_afl_vs_svt_flutter` | `heart_rate_bpm` | 139.316 | 183.230 | 2.072 | 0.709 |

Interpretation:

- Synthetic `svt_flutter` is too regular and too fast compared with MIT-BIH
  `SVT`/`AFL` windows.
- Synthetic `monomorphic_vt` is too wide and too low-entropy compared with
  MIT-BIH `VT` windows. MIT-BIH VT runs are short and often not as cleanly
  wide-complex as the current synthetic preset.
- Synthetic `vf_like` is too high-frequency and too complex compared with the
  MIT-BIH `VFL` windows extracted here.

## Next Fixes

1. Add explicit synthetic scenarios for real MIT-BIH labels that are currently
   unmatched: `AFIB`, ventricular bigeminy, ventricular trigeminy, `IVR`, and
   nodal rhythm.
2. Tune `svt_flutter` to allow more RR variability and lower average rate.
3. Tune `monomorphic_vt` using MIT-BIH/CUDB VT feature distributions instead of
   fixed wide-complex assumptions alone.
4. Tune `vf_like` separately for MIT-BIH `VFL` and CUDB/VFDB fibrillation,
   because MIT-BIH `VFL` windows are not the same target as chaotic VF.
